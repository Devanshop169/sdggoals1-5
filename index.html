Sustainable Development Goal 1 aims to eradicate extreme poverty by 2030. It focuses on ensuring all people have equal rights to economic resources, access to basic services, and social protection systems. This goal also addresses the resilience of the poor and those in vulnerable situations to economic, social, and environmental shocks.

Sources: United Nations, Global Goals

Sustainable Development Goal 2 aims to end hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture by 2030. It focuses on ensuring access to sufficient, safe, and nutritious food for all, eradicating all forms of malnutrition, and increasing agricultural productivity and sustainability, especially for small-scale food producers.

Sources: United Nations, Global Goals

SDG Goal 3 aims to ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all, at all ages. It targets reducing maternal mortality, ending epidemics like HIV/AIDS, and providing universal access to healthcare services. Goal 3 seeks to address major health challenges globally, fostering a world where everyone can thrive physically and mentally.

SDG Goal 4 strives to ensure inclusive and equitable quality education for all, promoting lifelong learning opportunities. It targets increasing literacy rates, improving access to vocational training, and enhancing educational facilities. Goal 4 aims to empower individuals, fostering a skilled workforce and enabling socioeconomic development worldwide.

SDG Goal 5 advocates for achieving gender equality and empowering all women and girls. It aims to end discrimination, violence, and harmful practices against women, ensuring equal opportunities in leadership and decision-making roles. Goal 5 envisions a world where gender equity prevails, fostering sustainable development and social progress.
